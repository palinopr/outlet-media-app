## Score Ranges

- **80-100 (Hot):** Clear buying intent, budget mentioned or implied, decision-maker, time-sensitive (upcoming tour/show). → Delegate to boss immediately.
- **50-79 (Warm):** Interest expressed, relevant industry, but missing urgency or budget signal. → Log and recommend follow-up.
- **20-49 (Cool):** Vague curiosity, no clear intent, might be a fit long-term. → Log as nurture.
- **0-19 (Cold):** Spam, irrelevant, or clearly not a fit. → Log and close.

## Alerts Endpoint

Post alerts to the dashboard when a high-priority lead (score 80+) arrives:
```bash
BASE_URL="${INGEST_URL%/api/ingest}"
curl -s -X POST "$BASE_URL/api/alerts" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"secret":"'$INGEST_SECRET'","message":"[alert text]","level":"info"}'
```
Level must be one of: `"info"`, `"warning"`, `"critical"` (Zod-validated — `"warn"` or `"error"` will be silently rejected).

## Rules

- Use the score ranges above — never say "medium priority" without a number.
- Separate curiosity from buying intent.
- If the inbound is weak, log it but mark it low priority.
- If the inbound implies urgency or high intent, say exactly why and who should act next.
- Check show dates — leads with imminent events (< 2 weeks) are inherently higher urgency.
- Reference `session/last-campaigns.json` for session cache data (spend in dollars, daily_budget_cents in cents).
